# Financial Manual

## Overview

This manual explains financial and reception workflows in ClinicFlow, including appointment intake, invoicing, payments, and accounting tasks.

## Roles and responsibilities

- **Receptionist**: Manages patient check-in, appointment scheduling, and initial billing capture.
- **Accountant**: Handles invoicing, payment reconciliation, reporting, and financial review.

## Receptionist workflow

### Patient registration

- Enter patient demographic, insurance, and contact details.
- Verify that the patient is associated with the correct clinic tenant.
- Create or update patient records as needed.

### Appointment handling

- Confirm patient appointments for the day.
- Register patient arrival and route them to the appropriate clinician.
- Update appointment status when the patient has been checked in.

### Billing intake

- Capture visit type, service category, and payment status.
- Record any copay or deposit paid at the clinic.
- Provide the patient with a summary of expected charges.

## Accountant workflow

### Invoice management

- Review completed clinic encounters and generate invoices.
- Ensure each invoice is linked to the correct patient and clinic tenant.
- Track invoice status, including paid, pending, or overdue.

### Payment tracking

- Reconcile cash, card, or transfer payments with clinic records.
- Mark invoices as paid once payment has been confirmed.
- Produce summary reports for daily, weekly, and monthly revenue.

### Financial reporting

- Monitor clinic income and outstanding balances.
- Generate reports for reconciliation and regulatory review.
- Support tax and compliance workflows by retaining accurate billing records.

## Recommended financial controls

- Use separate roles for reception and accounting to reduce fraud risk.
- Require manager review for discounts, refunds, and write-offs.
- Keep financial data access restricted to authorized users only.

## Data privacy and billing

- Protect patient financial information as part of overall patient data security.
- Only use the billing system for transactions tied to the clinic tenant.
- Align receipts, invoices, and financial records with applicable healthcare regulations.

## Practical notes

- If the current application does not yet expose billing screens, plan to add a dedicated financial module.
- Maintain a clear audit trail for all patient-facing payments and receipts.
- Coordinate with clinic management when creating clinic price lists and service definitions.
